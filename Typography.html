Typography

Here are the typeography posters showcasing each typographic system.

In Axial designs, typography follows an imaginary line. From this line, text can be seperated into collums of any width. Here I have created a line with my two contrasting colours, black and white.

Bilateral typography, unlike Axial designs are on the line or pivot rather than following an axis. For this design I have created a tilt on which the text falls and included shapes which follow this tilt.

Dilatational typography is where text follows the ouside of a circle. In this design I have put circles inside of other circles to make a unique dilatational design.

Grid typography is where the text is seperated into a grid format. Here I have made a simple 2x2 grid, where there are four boxes. I have used contrasting colours of black and white to create a contrast between different parts of the text.

Radial typeography involves text radiating from a center point. As we could only use one extra colour for the design, I simply changed the opacity of my layered circles creating the impression of a gradient of colours.

Random typeography follows no structure or "rules". Here I have used 3D objects, made text appear 3D, used blurs and reapeated text to create a completely random design.

Transitional typeography captures the feeling of natural movement. In this case I have tried to represent the movement of the wind, and used circles to aid the feeling of movement.
